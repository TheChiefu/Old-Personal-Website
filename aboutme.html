An Overview:

Hello, I am Austin Lanphear; also known online through my aliases as The Chiefu, thechief120, AustinAwsome, and and many numerous in-game names I go by. I am a 17 year old male who love video games, computers, nature, and orchestral music; these are hobbies that are intertwined with one another. My love for computers and gaming came at an early age, every since I could remember I was always playing video games and using the family computer. Over time this interest grew, I wanted to create new things on my computer and I wanted to make video games on them; as time when on I knew how the basics of 3D Modeling and Level Designing, using tools such as Unity and Blender. As I grew older I wanted to self teach myself new ideas, so I started to learn HTML, CSS, some Javascript, C#, and as a normal language Japanese. The want of learning these languages stemed from my interest in wanting to make video games, while wanting to learn Japanese comes from my interesting in wanting to move to Japan, but thats a story for later.

So because I wanted to move abroad I started self-teaching myself during the summer at Memrise where I learned Hirigana「ひりがな」, Katakana「カタカナ」, and some Kanji「漢字」; the basis of the Japanese language. My reasoning for my want to move to Japan came from numerous sources, one being that a large amount of games I played origined from Japan, my favorite shows came from Japan, and my interest in wanting to learn new language; another reasoning being that I've always wanted to move to a different country. An example sentence that I constructed using only the knowledge I self taught myself would be: こんにちは私はオースティンですはじめまして。ぼくは学習日本語好きです！じゃまた。 Meaning, "Hello my name is Austin nice to meet you. I like learning Japanese! See you later", in such case I used the formal 私 "I" instead of the informal "ぼく" Male or Tomboy usage of "I" in the case of keeping formality; yet at the end I say "じやまた" which is an informal way of saying "see you later" because although it's informal I like to be seen as a mix between formal and friendly. By keeping the respect by using formal language at first than transitioning into friendly or less strict language essentially states that I don't want to be seen as strict or stern.
